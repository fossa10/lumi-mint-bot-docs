---
title: 'Proxy Setup'
description: 'Using a proxy to avoid rate limits during eligibility checks'
---

A proxy routes some of the bot's network requests through a different IP address instead of your own. This helps avoid rate limits when checking eligibility across many wallets at once.

<Note>
  The proxy only covers OpenSea sign-in and eligibility checks. The actual mint transaction always goes out directly, see the warning in [Configuring the Bot](/configuration#proxy).
</Note>

## Minimum recommended setup

**Rotating residential proxies.** These route through real home IP addresses and rotate between them, which is enough for most botters.

For higher performance (and higher cost), **ISP proxies** are faster and more stable, since they don't hop between IPs as much, but they cost more per proxy.

## Providers

I personally use [Data Impulse](https://dataimpulse.com/) Resi Premium. Other options:

- [statproxies.com](https://www.statproxies.com/)
- [birdproxies.com](https://birdproxies.com/en)

<Tip>
  Search Google or ask an AI chatbot for current proxy providers and pricing before committing to one.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Pulse Anchor doesn't endorse or partner with any of these providers. Do your own research before buying.
</Warning>

## Adding your proxy to the config

Once you have proxy credentials, fill in the `proxy` section of your settings file, see the field reference in [Configuring the Bot](/configuration#proxy).

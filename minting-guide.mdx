OpenSea, Blever, Transient, and Scatter all follow the same basic flow. This page walks through it once, and platform-specific differences are called out where they matter. If you're minting from a contract that isn't on any of these platforms, see [Custom Contract Mode](/custom-contract) instead.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fill in the drop details">
    In your settings file, set `mint.slug` (the collection slug from the drop's URL) and `mint.chain`. See [Configuring the Bot](/configuration#mint-settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the platform from the main menu">
    Choose **OpenSea**, **Blever**, **Transient**, or **Scatter** from the [main menu](/main-menu). The bot fetches the drop's details automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your wallets">
    A checklist of your configured wallets appears. Pick which ones you want to mint with. See [Setting Up Your Wallets](/wallet-setup#selecting-wallets-when-minting) for the keyboard controls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a stage or phase">
    The bot lists every sale stage, with its price, time window, and per-wallet limit:

    ```
      [0] Allowlist          - Jul 10, 9:00 AM  -> Jul 10, 10:00 AM  - 0.05 ETH  - AL only  - max 2/wallet
      [1] Public Sale        - Jul 10, 10:00 AM -> -                 - 0.08 ETH  - Public   - max 5/wallet
    ```

    Pick the stage you're eligible for and want to mint in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for eligibility checks">
    The bot signs into the platform with each selected wallet (a free, gasless "Sign-In With Ethereum" signature, not a transaction) and checks each wallet's remaining allowance for the stage you picked. If a wallet isn't eligible for an allowlist stage, it's excluded automatically.

    <Note>
      Your `mint.quantity_per_wallet` (or a per-wallet override) is a request, not a guarantee. If the stage's own per-wallet limit is lower than what you configured, the bot automatically caps your quantity down to match, and prints a warning when it does. If a wallet already minted some units earlier, the bot also subtracts those from what it will try to mint this time, so you never accidentally attempt more than a wallet is actually allowed. This check runs again right before the stage opens, in case the project changes the limit at the last minute.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a mint mode">
    Finally, pick **how** you want to mint: right now, at a scheduled time, waiting for cheap gas, or just a dry run. See [Mint Modes Explained](/mint-modes) for what each one does and when to use it.

    <Frame caption="The mode selection menu">
      <img src="/images/mint-modes.png" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional: auto-send minted NFTs">
    If you set `send_to_address` in your config, the bot asks whether to send each wallet's mint to that address right after it confirms. You can accept the default, type a different address for this run, or leave it blank to keep NFTs on the minting wallets. See `send_to_address` in [Configuring the Bot](/configuration#api-keys).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Platform differences

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="OpenSea">
    Available modes: Scheduled, Manual, Gas Monitor, Dry run, and MEV Tip (Ethereum only).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Blever">
    Available modes: Scheduled, Manual, Gas Monitor, Dry run, and MEV Tip (Ethereum only).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transient">
    Available modes: Scheduled, Manual, and Dry run only. **No Gas Monitor or MEV Tip modes** for Transient drops.

    Transient also charges a separate **protocol fee** on top of the mint price. If your `mint.mint_price_eth` doesn't cover that fee, the bot will warn you and ask for confirmation before continuing:

    ```
    This drop charges an additional platform fee of 0.0009 ETH/NFT.
    Proceed anyway (pay the extra fee)?
    ```

    Read this prompt carefully. Confirming means you'll pay more than `mint_price_eth` alone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Scatter">
    Available modes: Scheduled, Manual, and Dry run only. **No Gas Monitor or MEV Tip modes** for Scatter drops.

    One quirk: a merkle-gated (allowlist) invite list only appears in the list-selection menu if at least one of your loaded wallets is actually eligible for it. Scatter has no way to show gated lists you don't qualify for ahead of time. Public lists always show.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## After the mint

If you set `discord_webhook_url` in your config, a summary is posted to your Discord webhook automatically. Otherwise, results print directly in the terminal: one line per wallet showing the transaction hash and whether it confirmed or reverted.

<Warning>
  With Flashbots Protect on, the bot only waits 40 seconds for your transaction to land before it logs `FAILED`. That log line can mean the transaction genuinely failed, or it can just mean it hadn't been mined yet when the bot stopped waiting. If you see `FAILED` while using Flashbots Protect, check the transaction on a block explorer before assuming it didn't go through. It may still get mined after the 40-second mark.
</Warning>

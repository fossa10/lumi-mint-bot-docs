A VPS (virtual private server) is a rented computer that runs 24/7 in a data center. Botters use one instead of their own PC for a few reasons:

- **Lower latency.** A VPS sits closer to the RPC and platform servers the bot talks to, so your requests round-trip faster during a competitive mint.
- **Reliable uptime.** It keeps running even if your home internet drops or your PC restarts, and doesn't tie up your own computer while it's minting.
- **Isolation.** Running any third-party executable, this one included, on a dedicated VPS instead of your main PC limits what it could ever touch to that one machine, not your personal files, photos, saved passwords, or anything else on your day-to-day computer.

## Minimum recommended setup

- **OS:** Windows
- **Plan:** 8 CPUs, 24 GB RAM, 200 GB NVMe
- **Region:** US East

<Note>
  Pick a region close to the RPC endpoints and platforms you mint on. US East is a safe default for most Ethereum/OpenSea mints.
</Note>

## Providers

I personally use [Sauce Servers](https://sauceservers.com/). Other options:

- Cheaper: [Contabo](https://contabo.com/)
- More expensive: [Zesty](https://zestystore.com/)

<Tip>
  Search Google or ask an AI chatbot for current VPS providers and pricing before committing to one.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Pulse Anchor doesn't endorse or partner with any of these providers. Do your own research before renting one.
</Warning>

## Getting the bot running on your VPS

Once your VPS is set up, connect to it with Remote Desktop (built into Windows) and set up the bot exactly like you would on your own PC, see [Installation & First Run](/installation). Your license key still locks to that VPS as its own machine, so keep that in mind if you plan to switch servers later.

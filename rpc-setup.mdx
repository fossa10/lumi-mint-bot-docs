---

The free public RPCs in the default config work for most users, but they're shared with everyone else using them. During a busy mint, that can mean slower responses or hitting rate limits right when speed matters most. A paid RPC gives you a dedicated (or less crowded) connection instead.

## Billing

Most RPC providers bill **pay-as-you-go**: you get charged based on how much usage or traffic you actually send per month, not a flat fee.

## Providers

I personally use both [Alchemy](https://www.alchemy.com/) and [Ankr](https://www.ankr.com/). Other options:

- [dRPC](https://drpc.org/)
- [QuickNode](https://www.quicknode.com/)

<Tip>
  Search Google or ask an AI chatbot for current RPC providers and pricing before committing to one.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Pulse Anchor doesn't endorse or partner with any of these providers. Do your own research before buying.
</Warning>

## Adding your RPC to the config

Once you've created an endpoint with a provider, copy its URL and add it under the matching chain in the `rpcs` section of your settings file, see the field reference in [Configuring the Bot](/configuration#rpc-endpoints).

---
title: 'Setting Up Your Wallets'
description: 'How to give the bot access to your wallets'
---

The bot needs your wallet's **private key** to sign and submit mint transactions on your behalf.

<Warning title="Your private key = full control of your wallet">
  Anyone who gets your private key can move everything out of that wallet. Never share your settings file with anyone, never paste your private key into Discord/chat, and never commit these files to a public GitHub repo. Consider using a fresh "mint/burner wallet" that only holds what you need for minting, rather than your main wallet.

  Your private keys never leave your machine. Pulse Anchor will never see them.
</Warning>

In the `wallets` section of your settings file, add one line per wallet in the format:

```yaml
wallets:
  - main,0xabc123...yourprivatekeyhere
  - backup,0xdef456...anotherprivatekeyhere
```

- The part before the comma (`main`, `backup`) is a **label**, pick anything memorable, it's only shown to you in menus.
- The part after the comma is the wallet's private key, with or without the `0x` prefix.
- You can leave off the label entirely (just the key). The bot will label it with the first few characters of the wallet address instead.

<Tip>
  To find a wallet's private key in MetaMask: Account details → Show private key. Never do this on a shared or public screen.
</Tip>

## Selecting wallets when minting

Every time you start a mint, the bot shows a checklist of all your configured wallets:

```
Select wallets to use:
  ◉ main - 0x1a2b...9f8e
  ◯ backup - 0x3c4d...7a6b
```

- **Space** toggles a wallet
- **a** selects/deselects all
- **i** inverts the current selection
- **Enter** confirms

</Frame>

You must select at least one wallet to continue.

## Bulk wallet creation (coming soon)

Right now, wallets need to be created elsewhere (MetaMask or another wallet tool) before you add their private keys to your settings file. We're adding the ability to generate a batch of brand new wallets directly from within the bot, so you can spin up however many wallets you need without leaving the tool.
